WDD 131: Dynamic Web Fundamentals - Project Plan

Site Name

MW
Mindful Wellness Hub

The name "Mindful Wellness Hub" was chosen to reflect the site's focus on holistic health and well-being. The word "Mindful" emphasizes awareness and intentionality in health practices, while "Wellness" encompasses physical, mental, and emotional health. "Hub" suggests a central gathering place for resources, information, and community connection.

This name is memorable, clearly communicates the site's purpose, and appeals to individuals seeking a balanced, thoughtful approach to health and wellness. It positions the site as a comprehensive resource center rather than a single-focus platform.

Optional domain: mindful-wellness-hub.com

Site Purpose

The Mindful Wellness Hub serves as a comprehensive online resource for individuals seeking to improve their overall well-being through evidence-based practices and community support. The site provides practical tools, educational content, and connections to wellness resources.

Primary Services and Information:

The site offers guided meditation resources, wellness practice recommendations tailored to different lifestyles and goals, educational articles on mental and physical health topics, a directory of certified wellness practitioners, and an interactive community forum where members can share experiences and support one another. Additionally, the site provides a newsletter signup feature to deliver weekly wellness tips and updates directly to subscribers.

The target audience includes working professionals experiencing stress, individuals new to wellness practices seeking guidance, people managing anxiety or seeking mental health support, and anyone interested in developing sustainable healthy habits. The site aims to make wellness accessible, practical, and sustainable for everyday life.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: Stress Management for Beginners

Sarah is a 32-year-old marketing manager who has been experiencing high levels of work-related stress. She's heard about meditation but doesn't know where to start. She visits the site asking: "What are some simple, beginner-friendly meditation techniques I can practice during my lunch break?" She's looking for quick, practical techniques that don't require special equipment and can be done in 10-15 minutes.

Scenario 2: Finding Professional Support

Michael is a 45-year-old teacher who wants to find a certified wellness coach or therapist in his area who specializes in stress management and work-life balance. He asks: "How can I find qualified wellness practitioners near me, and what credentials should I look for?" He needs clear guidance on professional qualifications and wants verified contact information and specialties.

Scenario 3: Building Sustainable Habits

Emma is a 28-year-old software developer who has tried multiple wellness routines but struggles to maintain them long-term. She's asking: "What are realistic wellness practices I can incorporate into a busy schedule, and how do I stay consistent?" She needs practical advice on habit formation and realistic expectations for building lasting wellness practices.

Color Schema

The color palette for Mindful Wellness Hub was carefully selected to evoke feelings of calm, growth, and natural balance while maintaining professional credibility and accessibility.

Primary Color: Forest Green

Hex: #2C5F4F

Usage: Main headings, navigation bar, footer background, and primary buttons. This deep green conveys growth, stability, and natural wellness.

Secondary Color: Warm Sand

Hex: #D4A373

Usage: Accent elements, highlights, call-to-action buttons, and decorative borders. This warm, earthy tone adds approachability and complements the primary color.

Accent Color: Sage Green

Hex: #8B9A7E

Usage: Secondary headings, links, icon backgrounds, and subtle dividers. This softer green creates visual hierarchy and maintains the natural, calming theme.

Additional Supporting Colors:

Background Light (#F8F6F3) for page backgrounds and card elements, Text Dark (#2B2B2B) for body text and primary content, and Text Light (#5A5A5A) for secondary text and descriptions. These colors ensure excellent readability and pass WCAG accessibility standards for contrast ratios.

Typography

The typography system combines elegance with readability, using two carefully selected Google Fonts that complement the wellness theme while maintaining professional standards.

Playfair Display - Heading Font
Lato - Body Text Font

Playfair Display (Serif) - Used for all headings (h1, h2, h3) and the site logo. This elegant serif font adds sophistication and establishes visual hierarchy. Font weights: 600 (semi-bold) and 700 (bold). The high-contrast letterforms create distinction and memorable branding.

Lato (Sans-serif) - Used for body text, navigation links, buttons, and form labels. This highly legible sans-serif font ensures comfortable reading across all devices. Font weights: 300 (light) for subtle text, 400 (regular) for body content, and 700 (bold) for emphasis. Its humanist design feels friendly and accessible.

Typography Guidelines:

Headings will use Playfair Display to create strong visual impact and establish clear content hierarchy. Body text will use Lato for optimal readability and professional appearance. Line height is set to 1.7 for comfortable reading, and font sizes are responsive, scaling appropriately for mobile and desktop views. All text maintains WCAG AA accessibility standards for contrast against backgrounds.

Wireframes

The following wireframes illustrate the layout structure for the home page in both mobile and desktop views. These represent the general content organization and responsive design approach.

Mobile View (320px - 768px)

Single-column layout with stacked content cards for easy mobile navigation. Header contains logo and hamburger menu. Hero section features main call-to-action. Content cards stack vertically for optimal mobile viewing.

Desktop View (769px+)

Multi-column grid layout utilizing horizontal space. Header contains full navigation menu. Hero section spans full width with prominent imagery. Content cards display in three-column grid for efficient information presentation.

Layout Components:

Header includes site logo and navigation menu (responsive hamburger menu on mobile). Hero section features a welcoming headline, brief introduction, and primary call-to-action button. Main content area displays three featured sections such as meditation resources, wellness articles, and community highlights. Footer contains copyright information, social media links, and additional navigation.
